Building an Internet facing API

By default, services you build with Namespace are only exposed to other services which are co-deployed. Namespace ensures that a combination of networking and authentication/authorization policies are in place, to guarantee that.

But in order to be usable by your web application, or mobile application, at least one of your APIs will have to be Internet-facing. When you do, Namespace sets up:

  • An environment-scoped public DNS domain name that routes traffic from your Internet to your service endpoint. The domain name will be of the form {service}.{environment}-{username}.nscloud.dev.
  • A Lets Encrypt TLS certificate for that domain. And the certificate renewal is managed for you as well, on subsequent deployments.
  • A TLS-terminating ingress: that is, Namespace sets it up so that the traffic encryption and authentication policies between users and your services are distinct from the policies applied within your deployment.

Creating an Internet-facing endpoint is as simple as updating your service.cue with:

service: fn.#Service & {
    // exportService, ...

    ingress:              "INTERNET_FACING"
Login required

The Namespace-managed DNS and TLS capabilities are free to use, but require you to be logged in. Run ns login to authenticate with your Github account.

And run

ns deploy
Applies to all services

The ingress configuration above applies to all APIs exported by a service. If you want to separate your private and public APIs, create a separate service for that purpose.
